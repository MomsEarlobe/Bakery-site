# Golden Crust Bakery — Content & Copy Rules

## Voice Summary
Warm, friendly, genuine, slightly proud of the craft. Like a baker inviting you to try something fresh from the oven.

## Headline Rules
- Headlines should create desire, warmth, or curiosity
- Lead with the sensory experience, not the brand
- Good: "Baked Fresh Every Morning" / "From Our Oven to Your Table"
- Bad: "Welcome to Golden Crust Bakery" / "Your Artisanal Baking Destination"
- Keep headlines short — 3-8 words for impact
- Section headlines can be slightly longer

## Body Copy Rules
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Easy to skim — use the inverted pyramid
- Lead with the most interesting or appetizing detail
- Write for someone scrolling on their phone
- No walls of text
- Line-height: generous (1.6+) for readability

## CTA Rules
- Be specific: "Order for Pickup" not "Get Started"
- Be inviting: "See Today's Menu" not "View Products"
- Be warm: "Join Our List" not "Subscribe Now"
- Primary CTA should always be visible or easy to reach
- Never more than 2 CTAs competing for attention in one section

### Suggested CTA Labels
| Purpose | Primary | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| Ordering | "Order for Pickup" | "See the Menu" |
| Menu browsing | "Browse the Menu" | "Today's Specials" |
| Email signup | "Stay in the Loop" | "Get Updates" |
| Location | "Get Directions" | "See Our Hours" |
| Gallery | "See Our Bakes" | — |
| General | "Come Visit Us" | "Learn More" |

## Menu Copy Rules

### Item Names
- Clear and appetizing — no obscure culinary terms
- If the name isn't self-explanatory, the description should clarify
- Use descriptive modifiers: "Honey Oat Sourdough" not just "Sourdough"

### Item Descriptions
- 1-2 sentences max
- Lead with what makes it special or distinctive
- Include texture, flavor, or ingredient highlights
- Mention allergen/dietary info naturally when relevant
- Good: "Our signature sourdough with a 48-hour ferment. Crispy crust, tangy crumb, perfect for sandwiches."
- Bad: "A bread made with sourdough starter and baked in our ovens."

### Categories
- **Breads** — sourdough, whole grain, specialty loaves
- **Pastries** — croissants, danishes, scones, muffins
- **Seasonal** — rotating items tied to season or holiday
- **Drinks** — coffee, tea, fresh juice (if applicable)

## Testimonial Rules
- Write realistic, believable testimonials
- Mix long and short — some detailed, some punchy
- Include first name and context (e.g., "Sarah M., Sioux Falls")
- Focus on specific details: the bread, the atmosphere, the pickup experience
- Good: "The sourdough here ruined every other bread for me. I pick up a loaf every Saturday."
- Bad: "Great bakery! Highly recommend! 5 stars!"

## Email Signup Copy
- Promise specific value: new items, seasonal menus, special offers
- Make it feel like insider access, not spam
- Good: "Be the first to know when seasonal items drop. New bakes, special offers, and bakery news — straight to your inbox."
- Bad: "Subscribe to our newsletter for updates."

## FAQ Copy
- Write questions as real customers would ask them
- Keep answers concise and helpful
- Include: ordering process, pickup timing, dietary info, custom orders, location/parking
- Mark any answers that are unconfirmed as [PLACEHOLDER]

## Words and Phrases

### Use Often
- Fresh, handcrafted, baked daily, from scratch
- Golden, warm, crusty, tender, flaky, buttery
- Neighborhood, local, community, Sioux Falls
- Every morning, small batch, made by hand
- Order, pick up, taste, try, enjoy

### Use Sparingly
- Artisanal (once max — show it, don't label it)
- Premium (imply it through quality, don't state it)
- Luxury (the pricing is mid-tier — don't oversell)

### Never Use
- "Curated" / "innovative" / "cutting-edge" / "solutions"
- "We are passionate about..." (show passion through craft details)
- "A symphony of flavors" / "a culinary journey" (too precious)
- "Click here" / "Submit" (use specific action labels)
- Generic filler that could describe any business

## Placeholder Protocol
- Mark unconfirmed details with [PLACEHOLDER]
- Include a note about what information is needed
- Example: "[PLACEHOLDER: specific hours — confirm with business owner]"
- Never invent specific facts that could be wrong
